Data Visualization

Why Visualize Data?

Great infographics are not just aesthetically beautiful; they offer insight and new understanding. We design compelling visualizations that help you tell your story and captivate your audience.

The UAKN commissioned us to design an interactive visualization to explore essential data on Canada's young and growing urban Aboriginal population. The tool uses on data generated from the 2011 National Household Survey and Statistics Canada’s 2012 Aboriginal Peoples Survey.

UOIT's Digital Competency Profiler is a tool that provides a snapshot of how you use digital technology in your life and how skilled you feel about using it. Part of the development included the design of a visual profile that provides insight into the digital competencies that are foundational elements in successful careers in academia, commerce, service, or entrepreneurship.

This interactive visualization was designed to explore Pierre Lévy's semantic meta-language IEML. The spheres consist of paradigmatic tables of semantically related expressions. Although not as practical as a 2D table, the spherical projections help illustrate the concepts of the "semantic sphere". It has been used in various publications and presentations about the technology.
